1. PURPOSE AND OBJECTIVES

This Memorandum of Understanding ("MOU") establishes a cooperative relationship between the Agency and the Organization for the planning, development, construction, and maintenance of recreational trails on public lands managed by the Agency.

The objectives of this MOU are to:

  • Develop sustainable recreational trails that enhance public enjoyment of public lands
  • Establish clear roles and responsibilities for trail planning, construction, and maintenance
  • Promote environmental stewardship and conservation
  • Engage community volunteers in public land management
  • Provide diverse recreational opportunities for the public

4. AGENCY RESPONSIBILITIES

The Agency agrees to:

4.1 Planning and Approval

  • Review and process trail proposals in a timely manner
  • Conduct necessary environmental reviews and assessments
  • Issue required permits and authorizations
  • Provide maps, GIS data, and other relevant information

4.2 Technical Assistance

  • Provide technical guidance on trail design and construction
  • Assist with identifying and addressing environmental concerns
  • Advise on compliance with applicable laws and regulations

4.3 Resources

  • Provide access to the project area for authorized activities
  • Consider requests for material resources when available
  • Recognize volunteer hours as in-kind contributions for grant matching purposes

5. ORGANIZATION RESPONSIBILITIES

The Organization agrees to:

5.1 Planning and Design

  • Develop trail proposals consistent with Agency management plans
  • Design trails according to sustainable standards
  • Submit detailed plans for Agency review and approval
  • Obtain all necessary permits and authorizations

5.2 Construction

  • Construct trails according to approved plans and specifications
  • Use sustainable trail building techniques
  • Minimize environmental impacts
  • Provide qualified supervision for volunteer work
  • Document and report construction activities

5.3 Maintenance

  • Perform regular trail inspections
  • Conduct routine and seasonal maintenance
  • Address safety hazards promptly
  • Organize volunteer maintenance events
  • Document and report maintenance activities

5.4 Volunteer Management

  • Recruit, train, and supervise volunteers
  • Ensure volunteers sign appropriate waivers
  • Track and report volunteer hours
  • Provide safety training and equipment

5.5 Funding

  • Seek grants and donations to support trail projects
  • Manage project budgets responsibly
  • Provide financial reports as required

6. JOINT RESPONSIBILITIES

Both parties agree to:

6.1 Communication

  • Designate primary points of contact
  • Hold regular coordination meetings
  • Provide timely updates on project status
  • Address issues collaboratively

6.2 Public Outreach

  • Coordinate public communications
  • Acknowledge partnership in publications and signage
  • Conduct joint public meetings when appropriate
  • Promote responsible trail use

6.3 Evaluation

  • Jointly evaluate trail conditions and usage
  • Review partnership effectiveness annually
  • Adapt management approaches as needed

7. TRAIL STANDARDS AND SPECIFICATIONS

7.1 Design Standards

All trails shall be designed according to:

  • [Applicable Agency standards]
  • International Mountain Bicycling Association (IMBA) Trail Standards
  • Universal Trail Assessment Process (UTAP) guidelines where applicable
  • [Other relevant standards]

7.2 Sustainability Requirements

Trail design and construction shall incorporate:

  • Sustainable grades and alignments
  • Effective drainage features
  • Erosion control measures
  • Minimal impact to sensitive resources
  • Climate resilience considerations

7.3 Signage

Trail signage shall conform to Agency standards and include:

  • Trailhead information boards
  • Wayfinding markers
  • Regulatory and warning signs
  • Interpretive information where appropriate

8. PROJECT APPROVAL PROCESS

8.1 Proposal Submission

The Organization shall submit trail proposals including:

  • Detailed maps and alignments
  • Design specifications
  • Construction methods
  • Timeline and phasing
  • Maintenance plan
  • Budget and funding sources

8.2 Agency Review

The Agency shall:

  • Review proposals for consistency with management plans
  • Conduct site visits and field reviews
  • Identify any concerns or required modifications
  • Determine level of environmental analysis required

8.3 Authorization

Upon approval, the Agency shall issue:

  • Written authorization to proceed
  • Specific permits as required
  • Any special conditions or mitigation requirements

9. LIABILITY AND INSURANCE

9.1 Insurance Requirements

The Organization shall maintain:

  • General liability insurance with minimum coverage of $[AMOUNT]
  • Volunteer accident insurance
  • [Other required insurance]

The Organization shall provide certificates of insurance naming the Agency as an additional insured.

9.2 Volunteer Protection

The Organization shall ensure all volunteers:

  • Sign Agency-approved volunteer agreements
  • Receive appropriate safety training
  • Use required personal protective equipment
  • Work within the scope of authorized activities

9.3 Indemnification

The Organization agrees to indemnify the Agency to the extent allowed by law for claims arising from Organization activities, except where caused by Agency negligence.

10. TERMINATION

10.1 Termination by Notice

Either party may terminate this MOU with [NUMBER] days written notice.

10.2 Termination for Cause

This MOU may be terminated immediately for material breach of its terms.

10.3 Effect of Termination

Upon termination:

  • Ongoing construction shall be halted or completed as mutually agreed
  • The Organization shall remove temporary structures and equipment
  • The Agency shall assume responsibility for completed trails
  • Both parties shall meet to determine final disposition of projects

11. FUNDING AND FISCAL CONSIDERATIONS

11.1 No Financial Obligation

This MOU does not obligate funds from either party.

11.2 Separate Agreements

Specific projects requiring funding shall be addressed through separate agreements.

11.3 Grants and Donations

The Organization may apply for grants and accept donations to support trail projects with Agency approval.

12. MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS

12.1 Non-Exclusive Agreement

This MOU is non-exclusive and does not preclude either party from entering similar agreements with other entities.

12.2 Compliance with Laws

All activities shall comply with applicable federal, state, and local laws and regulations.

12.3 Modification

This MOU may only be modified by written amendment signed by both parties.

12.4 No Third-Party Beneficiaries

This MOU does not create any rights or benefits for third parties.

12.5 Dispute Resolution

Disputes shall be resolved through good faith negotiation between the parties.

12.6 Public Information

This MOU and related documents may be subject to public disclosure laws.
