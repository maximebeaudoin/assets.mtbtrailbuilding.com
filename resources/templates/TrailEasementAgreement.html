RECITALS:

WHEREAS, Grantor is the owner in fee simple of certain real property located in [COUNTY] County, [STATE], more particularly described in Exhibit A attached hereto and incorporated herein by reference (the "Property");

WHEREAS, the Property possesses natural, scenic, recreational, and/or open space values (collectively, "Conservation Values") of great importance to Grantor, Grantee, and the people of [STATE];

WHEREAS, Grantor desires to grant to Grantee a permanent easement for the purpose of establishing, constructing, maintaining, and managing a public recreational trail across a portion of the Property;

WHEREAS, Grantee is a [TYPE OF ORGANIZATION: e.g., nonprofit corporation, land trust, government entity] qualified to hold and enforce conservation easements under the laws of the State of [STATE];

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants, terms, conditions, and restrictions contained herein, and pursuant to the laws of the State of [STATE], Grantor hereby voluntarily grants and conveys to Grantee a perpetual Trail Easement over the Property of the nature and character and to the extent hereinafter set forth.

1. GRANT OF EASEMENT

Grantor hereby grants and conveys to Grantee, its successors and assigns, a perpetual, non-exclusive easement over, under, and across that portion of the Property described and depicted in Exhibit B attached hereto (the "Easement Area"), for the purposes of:

  1. Constructing, maintaining, repairing, and managing a recreational trail for public use;
  2. Installing and maintaining trail-related improvements including bridges, culverts, drainage structures, signage, and other trail amenities;
  3. Accessing the Easement Area as necessary to construct, inspect, maintain, and repair the trail and related improvements; and
  4. Permitting public access for recreational trail use subject to the terms and conditions set forth herein.

2. EASEMENT AREA

The Easement Area shall consist of a corridor [NUMBER] feet in width, the centerline of which is described in Exhibit B. The Easement Area may be relocated only with the mutual written consent of both Grantor and Grantee.

3. PERMITTED USES

3.1 Grantee's Rights

Grantee shall have the following rights within the Easement Area:

  1. To construct, maintain, repair, and manage a recreational trail and related improvements;
  2. To remove vegetation, rocks, soil, or other materials as reasonably necessary for trail construction and maintenance;
  3. To install and maintain signage, including directional, interpretive, and regulatory signs;
  4. To construct and maintain bridges, culverts, water bars, steps, and other trail structures;
  5. To enter upon the Easement Area at reasonable times to inspect, maintain, and repair the trail;
  6. To permit public access for recreational trail use as specified in Section 4; and
  7. To enforce rules and regulations governing public use of the trail.

3.2 Grantor's Reserved Rights

Grantor reserves all rights as owner of the Property, including the right to use and enjoy the Property in any manner not inconsistent with this Agreement, including but not limited to:

  1. The right to sell, transfer, lease, or otherwise convey the Property, subject to this Agreement;
  2. The right to cross the Easement Area at any point for access to other portions of the Property;
  3. The right to maintain existing roads, utilities, and other improvements that cross the Easement Area; and
  4. The right to use the Easement Area for purposes that do not materially interfere with the trail or public access.

4. PUBLIC ACCESS

4.1 Permitted Public Uses

The trail shall be open to the public for the following non-motorized recreational uses:

  • [List permitted uses, e.g., hiking, mountain biking, cross-country skiing, etc.]

4.2 Prohibited Public Uses

The following uses are expressly prohibited:

  • Motorized vehicles (except for maintenance, emergency, or mobility assistance devices)
  • Equestrian use (unless specifically permitted in writing)
  • Camping or overnight use
  • Fires or smoking
  • Hunting or discharge of firearms
  • [Other prohibited uses]

4.3 Hours of Access

Public access shall be limited to daylight hours, from sunrise to sunset, unless otherwise agreed upon in writing.

4.4 Temporary Closures

Grantor may temporarily close the trail for:

  • Forestry operations
  • Agricultural activities
  • Hunting (if applicable)
  • Other land management activities

Grantor shall provide at least [NUMBER] days' notice to Grantee of any planned closure. Grantee may temporarily close the trail for maintenance or safety concerns.

5. CONSTRUCTION AND MAINTENANCE

5.1 Initial Construction

Grantee shall construct the trail according to sustainable trail building standards as established by the International Mountain Bicycling Association (IMBA) or equivalent organization. Construction shall minimize environmental impact and erosion.

5.2 Trail Standards

The trail shall be constructed and maintained to the following standards:

  • Maximum width of [NUMBER] feet for the tread surface
  • Sustainable grades appropriate for the permitted uses
  • Proper drainage features to prevent erosion
  • Minimal disturbance to surrounding vegetation and soil

5.3 Maintenance Responsibilities

Grantee shall be responsible for:

  • Regular inspection of the trail
  • Routine maintenance including clearing fallen trees, repairing erosion damage, and managing vegetation
  • Maintaining trail signage
  • Addressing any safety concerns in a timely manner

5.4 Improvements

Grantee may install and maintain the following improvements within the Easement Area:

  • Trail markers and signage
  • Bridges, culverts, and drainage features
  • Benches or rest areas
  • [Other approved structures]

Any additional improvements not listed above require prior written approval from Grantor.

6. LIABILITY AND INSURANCE

6.1 Liability Protection

Grantee shall maintain general liability insurance with minimum coverage of $[AMOUNT] per occurrence, naming Grantor as an additional insured. Grantee shall provide proof of insurance annually.

6.2 Recreational Use Statute

Both parties acknowledge that this Agreement is intended to invoke the protections of [STATE]'s Recreational Use Statute, [CITATION], which limits the liability of landowners who make their land available for public recreational use without charge.

6.3 Indemnification

Grantee agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Grantor from any claims, damages, or liabilities arising from the construction, maintenance, or public use of the trail, except in cases of gross negligence or willful misconduct by Grantor.

7. ENFORCEMENT AND REMEDIES

7.1 Right to Enforce

Grantee shall have the right to enforce the terms of this Agreement and prevent any activity that is inconsistent with its purpose.

7.2 Notice of Violation

If either party becomes aware of a violation or potential violation of this Agreement, they shall promptly notify the other party in writing.

7.3 Remedies

In the event of a violation, the parties shall first attempt to resolve the matter through good faith negotiation. If such efforts fail, either party may seek injunctive relief, specific performance, or other appropriate remedies through legal action.

7.4 Costs of Enforcement

If legal action is necessary to enforce this Agreement, the prevailing party shall be entitled to recover reasonable attorney's fees and costs.

8. AMENDMENT AND TERMINATION

8.1 Amendment

This Agreement may be amended only with the written consent of both Grantor and Grantee. Any amendment shall be consistent with the purpose of this Agreement and shall be recorded in the public records.

8.2 Termination

This Agreement may be terminated only:

  1. By mutual written agreement of Grantor and Grantee;
  2. By judicial proceedings if circumstances render the purpose of this Agreement impossible to fulfill; or
  3. As provided by applicable law.
