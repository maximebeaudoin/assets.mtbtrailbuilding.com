4. TRAIL CONSTRUCTION AND MAINTENANCE

4.1 Trail Construction

The Organization shall have the right to construct recreational trails on the property as shown in the attached trail plan (Exhibit B). Any significant deviation from the approved trail plan requires written approval from the Landowner.

4.2 Construction Standards

All trails shall be constructed according to sustainable trail building standards as established by the International Mountain Bicycling Association (IMBA) or equivalent organization. Construction shall minimize environmental impact and erosion.

4.3 Maintenance Responsibilities

The Organization shall be responsible for:

  • Regular inspection of trails
  • Routine maintenance including clearing fallen trees, repairing erosion damage, and managing vegetation
  • Maintaining trail signage
  • Addressing any safety concerns in a timely manner

4.4 Improvements

The Organization may install and maintain the following improvements:

  • Trail markers and signage
  • Bridges, culverts, and drainage features
  • [Other approved structures]

Any additional improvements not listed above require prior written approval from the Landowner.

5. PUBLIC ACCESS

5.1 Permitted Uses

The trails may be used for the following recreational activities:

  • [List permitted activities, e.g., hiking, mountain biking, etc.]

5.2 Prohibited Uses

The following activities are expressly prohibited:

  • Motorized vehicles (except for maintenance, emergency, or as specifically permitted)
  • Hunting or discharge of firearms
  • Camping or fires
  • [Other prohibited activities]

5.3 Hours of Access

Public access shall be limited to daylight hours, from sunrise to sunset, unless otherwise agreed upon in writing.

5.4 Temporary Closures

The Landowner reserves the right to temporarily close trails for:

  • Forestry operations
  • Agricultural activities
  • Hunting seasons
  • Other land management activities

The Landowner shall provide at least [NUMBER] days' notice of any planned closure.

6. LIABILITY AND INSURANCE

6.1 Liability Protection

The Organization shall maintain general liability insurance with minimum coverage of $[AMOUNT] per occurrence, naming the Landowner as an additional insured. The Organization shall provide proof of insurance annually.

6.2 Recreational Use Statute

Both parties acknowledge that this Agreement is intended to invoke the protections of [STATE]'s Recreational Use Statute, [CITATION], which limits the liability of landowners who make their land available for public recreational use without charge.

6.3 Indemnification

The Organization agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Landowner from any claims, damages, or liabilities arising from the construction, maintenance, or public use of the trails, except in cases of gross negligence or willful misconduct by the Landowner.

7. SIGNAGE

7.1 Required Signage

The Organization shall install and maintain:

  • Trailhead signs indicating permitted uses and hours
  • Boundary markers where trails enter and exit the property
  • Warning signs for any hazardous features
  • Signs acknowledging the Landowner's contribution (if desired by Landowner)

7.2 Rules and Regulations

All signage shall clearly communicate applicable rules and regulations to trail users.

8. TERMINATION

8.1 Termination by Notice

Either party may terminate this Agreement with [NUMBER] days' written notice.

8.2 Termination for Cause

This Agreement may be terminated immediately for material breach of its terms.

8.3 Effect of Termination

Upon termination, the Organization shall:

  • Remove all signage and structures as requested by the Landowner
  • Restore the property to its original condition as reasonably practicable
  • Cease promoting or advertising the trails on the property

9. TRANSFER OF PROPERTY

If the Landowner sells or transfers the property, the Landowner shall:

  • Notify the Organization at least [NUMBER] days prior to transfer
  • Inform the new owner of this Agreement
  • Use reasonable efforts to secure the new owner's commitment to continue the Agreement
